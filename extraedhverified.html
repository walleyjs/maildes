  • Knowledge Box
    Monopoly
  • A monopolistic market is the diametric opposite of the perfectly competitive market. While the perfect competition has a "perfect" competition, a monopoly is characterized by no competition. Hence, there is only one seller in the market, ruling the market with an iron fist. There extremely high barriers to entry which may be legal (e.g. patent), as a result of large cost of producing the good, or due to natural resources available to no one else. Instead of the market forces, the monopoly has the power to choose its own prices, or total quantity of that product in the market (but not both). As a result, a pure monopoly is undesirable because it may decide to restrict output in such a way that people who need the product are unable to get it.
    That is theory though. In real life, pure monopolies rarely exist, because even where the monopoly is backed by law, there is usually a black market or a similar product as an alternative. However, even near-monopolies are able to achieve super normal gains . here
